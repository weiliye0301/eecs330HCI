Preparation Checklist


Hurricane winds can cause trees and branches to fall, so before hurricane season it is important to remove those branches to ensure your safety.

Durring the hurricane, heavy rain is very common and leaking water may damage your property.

Hurricane will bring damage to the structure of your house and you get no chance to repair them during the hurricane. Thus, it is important to check their stability before the disaster.

It is highly possible that the electricity will go out during the hurricane. If needed, a generator is essential during the blackout.

WARNING:

Remember to keep generators and other alternate power/heat sources outside, at least 20 feet away from windows and doors and protected from moisture; and NEVER try to power the house wiring by plugging a generator into a wall outlet.

If the scale of the hurricane is not large and you are not considering evacuating to a nearby shelter, building a standard "safe room" is an alternative.
